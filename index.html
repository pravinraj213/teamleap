Why I Started Building TeamLeap

Throughout my computer science degree, I saw so many projects suffer because people couldn't find good teammates. Whether it was group assignments, hackathons, or personal projects, the struggle was real.

I experienced it myself. Sometimes I got stuck with teammates who weren't engaged, or I couldn't find anyone with the specific skills I needed. It was frustrating, time-consuming, and often impacted the quality of our work.

I've also seen brilliant students miss out on great opportunities because they were introverted, or didn't know how to network effectively to find like-minded peers.

"Team projects and hackathons are crucial for learning and building a portfolio. But without the right team, they can quickly become a source of stress instead of growth."

That's why I started building TeamLeap. I wanted to create a platform that makes it easy for students to connect with peers who share their drive, skills, and commitment. A place where you can find not just group members, but actual collaborators who make the experience better.

Not just skills. TeamLeap is built on trust.

This isn't a job-matching platform. It's a people-matching one. TeamLeap is for students who:

Seek Reliable Collaborators

Connect with students genuinely committed to completing projects, reducing the risk of uneven workloads.

Have Specific Project Needs

Easily find peers with complementary skills, whether it's coding, design, research, or writing.

Value Team Dynamics

Go beyond skills; find teammates whose work ethic and communication style align with yours.

Want to Grow & Learn

Pair with mentors or mentees, expanding your knowledge and tackling more ambitious challenges together.

The real goal? Help you feel less alone — and more capable — in your journey through college.

Why TeamLeap?

These statistics and direct voices from both students and recruiters prove that team formation, communication breakdowns, and peer isolation are real, measurable problems. By solving these collaboration obstacles, TeamLeap empowers students for success in college projects, hackathons, and career launches.
